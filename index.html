  • Why is encryption important?
    Encryption is a crucial technology in modern cybersecurity, protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access. It ensures data confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity across various digital communications and storage systems.

    Value of Encryption

    Confidentiality: Encryption keeps data private by converting it into an unreadable format that only authorized users can decrypt. This is essential for protecting personal, financial, and business information.

    Data Integrity: Encryption ensures that data has not been altered during transmission or storage. If encrypted data is tampered with, it becomes unreadable upon decryption.

    Authentication: Encryption techniques, such as digital signatures and certificates, verify the identity of senders and prevent impersonation in online transactions.

    Compliance & Legal Requirements: Many organizations and industries are required by law to encrypt sensitive data. Regulations like GDPR, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS mandate encryption to protect user data and prevent cyber breaches.
